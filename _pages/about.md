Hi, I'm Subrat, glad you stopped by. This blog is my little corner of the internet where I share what I'm learning, building, and trying to understand a bit better.

I work as a data scientist, and most of what I write sits at the intersection of machine learning, generative AI, software, and the real-world systems that tie them together. That's where the data is messy, the problems are genuinely interesting, and the small details end up mattering more than they probably should. I like work that's both technical and practical: building things, pulling them apart, and figuring out what actually works. I've been tinkering with large language models since well before they became part of the zeitgeist, and I'm especially interested in how they can be applied thoughtfully to real problems.

When I'm not at a keyboard, you'll usually find me behind a camera. Landscape photography is probably the thing that pulls me furthest from screens, and I like it that way. It's a different kind of attention, with fewer dashboards and better views.

You'll find a mix here: technical notes, project write-ups, ideas I'm still working through, and the occasional post that has nothing to do with work and everything to do with curiosity. Thanks for reading.
