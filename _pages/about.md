Hi, I'm Subrat. I work as a data scientist in healthcare, and this blog is my corner of the internet for sharing what I'm learning, building, and trying to understand a little better.

A lot of what I write sits at the intersection of data science, software, and real-world systems, especially in healthcare, where the data is messy, the problems are interesting, and the details matter more than they probably should. I'm drawn to work that is both technical and practical: building things, breaking them down, and figuring out what actually works. I've also been working with large language models since well before they became part of the zeitgeist, and I'm especially interested in how they can be applied thoughtfully to real problems rather than just used to generate more internet.

Outside of work, I spend a lot of time behind a camera. Landscape photography is probably the thing that pulls me furthest away from screens, and I like that. It's a different way of paying attention, with fewer dashboards and better views.

This blog will mostly be a mix of technical notes, project write-ups, ideas I'm exploring, and the occasional post that has nothing to do with work and everything to do with curiosity.
