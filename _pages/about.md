Hi, I'm Subrat, glad you stopped by. This blog is my little corner of the internet where I share what I'm learning, building, and trying to understand a bit better.

My day job sits in healthcare data science, where I work on applied machine learning and the messy systems around real data products. Most of what I write lives at the same intersection: machine learning, generative AI, software, and the real-world systems that tie them together. That's where the data is messy, the problems are genuinely interesting, and the small details end up mattering more than they probably should. I've been interested in large language models for years, especially in how they can be used carefully on real problems.

When I'm not at a keyboard, you'll usually find me behind a camera. Landscape photography is probably the thing that pulls me furthest from screens, and I like it that way. It's a different kind of attention, with fewer dashboards and better views.

You'll find project write-ups, technical notes, experiments, and the occasional detour into whatever I'm trying to understand that week. Thanks for reading.
